Audience Size (Summary)

The sum of audience members (Facebook Friends/Fans, Google+ Followers, Twitter Followers, Instagram Followers, Weibo Followers, Pinterest Followers, LinkedIn Connections/Followers, and YouTube Channel Subscribers) for all accounts in the Initiatives. The percentage change (up or down) is calculated by comparing the data from the current date range to the data from the prior (equivalent) date range. For example, if the date range is 60 days, the percentage is calculated by comparing the current data to data from the previous 60 day period. Click the percentage to see each channel’s values for the current period, previous period, change (count) and change (percentage). Click on the channel to see the same data for each account. Engagement (Summary)

Engagement is the sum (count) of all engagement received by messages published during the date range, for all accounts in the Initiatives. Engagement consists of: All Bitly or BudURL clicks, Facebook stories, Twitter Favorites, Replies and Retweets, Weibo Comments (including Replies) and Reposts, LinkedIn Likes, Comments, and Shares, Google+ +1s, Reshares and Comments, Instagram Likes and Comments, Pinterest Repins, Comments, and Likes, YouTube Likes, Dislikes, Comments, Favorites, and Video Responses. The percentage change (up or down) is calculated by comparing the data from the current date range to the data from the prior (equivalent) date range. For example, if the date range is 60 days, the percentage is calculated by comparing the current data to data from the previous 60 day period. Click the percentage to see each channel’s values for the current period, previous period, change (count) and change (percentage). Click on the channel to see the same data for each account.These values are calculated by .

Date range reflects time of message publication
The date range defines when the message was published, not when the engagement was received. For example: you published a message a month ago. If you filter the data based on the week you published the message, the results return all engagement activity received for the message, over the entire month. This is because some messages, such as videos, can take a long time for their maximum engagement to be achieved.

No Engagement Metrics for Native Retweets
Though “native” Retweets posted through are counted as publishing activity in , engagement metrics are not available for them. (From Twitter’s perspective, a native Retweet is an additional instance of the original tweet, not a separate tweet that can have independent, measurable engagement. Any engagement on the Retweet would be credited to the original Tweet.)
So if you post a “native” Retweet using , and other people engage with the retweet, that engagement is not counted. Consequently, Average Engagement ratios may be skewed. If you must have engagement metrics on all retweets published through , limit users to Editable Retweets only.

The bar charts show a monthly total of audience membership, by channel. Click on a channel’s section of the bar graph to see audience data per account. Click on the name of the month to show audience counts for each channel on each day of the month. Click on a channel’s section of the bar graph to see audience by account. These values are reported by the respective channels.

The Change Since Prior Period graph shows the trend in audience count over multiple weeks; click on a week’s data point to see the sum for each channel as of a specific week. The percentage change (up or down) is calculated by comparing the data from the current date range to the data from the prior (equivalent) date range. For example, if the date range is 60 days, the percentage is calculated by comparing the current data to data from the previous 60 day period.The End of Period number is the most recent count; click on the number to see audience data for each channel. The End of Prior Period is the count on the last day of the prior period. These values are calculated by .

For example, if the date range is 60 days, the percentage is calculated by comparing the current data to data from the previous 60 day period. The [#-Day] Period number is the sum over the date range; click on the number to see publishing sums for each channel. The Prior [#-Days] number is the sum over the previous time period.
