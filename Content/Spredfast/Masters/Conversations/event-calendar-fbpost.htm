Metrics

The Metrics tab provides metrics data on each post, broken out by account. To change accounts, use the [Account name] drop-down menu (chevron icon) in the Message Details header. The frequency at which the metrics are updated depends upon the type of metric, and for Content metrics, the age of the item. See Content Metrics.

Total Value

The Total Value is calculated using the amounts (in points or currency) set per engagement type in Initiative Settings > Values.

Bitly Clicks

This number of clicks on an embedded link in the message. This number is reported by Bitly.

BudURL Clicks

This number of clicks on an embedded link in the message. This number is reported by Bitly.

Web Conversions

If you use Google Analytics to track conversion information for your site and have integrated it with , conversion counts and value data for the message are provided. Google Analytics data is updated every 24 hours.

Twitter Favorites Count Limitation
collects Favorites data for an account’s 3200 most recent tweets. Once a tweet falls outside that limit (Tweet 3201), its Favorites count is no longer updated. Over long periods of time, or for very active Twitter accounts, users may see a discrepancy between Twitter and Favorites counts.

Facebook Insights

  • For Facebook Pages only. Facebook Insights data is tracked by Facebook for 28 days after a message is published.
  • Impressions—The sum of all views of the message during the first 28 days. It can include multiple views by the same person. Impressions data is also classified as:
  • Organic—The number of people, fans or nonfans who saw this post in their News Feed, Ticker or on your Page.
  • Paid—The number of people who saw this post from a Facebook Ad or Sponsored Story.
  • Viral—The number of people who saw this post from a story published by a friend. These stories can include liking, commenting or sharing your post, or answering a question.
  • Reach—The number of unique people who saw the post.
  • Organic—The number of unique people, fans or nonfans who saw this post in their News Feed, Ticker or on your Page.
  • Paid—The number of unique people who saw this post from a Facebook Ad or Sponsored Story.
  • Viral—The number of unique people who saw this post from a story published by a friend. These stories can include liking, commenting or sharing your post, or answering a question.
  • Engaged Users—The number of unique people who have clicked anywhere on your post during the first 28 days. This count includes both storytellers (those who have liked, commented, shared, or answered a question) plus consumers (those who have played a video or clicked a link or photo). A single user’s multiple actions are only counted once; if a person liked the post and clicked a link, the Engaged User count only increases by one.
  • Engagement Rate—The number of people who clicked on, liked, commented on, or shared a post (post_engaged_users) divided by the number of unique people who saw it (post_impressions_unique).
  • Engagement Details
  • Likes, Comments and Shares—The number of Likes, Comments and Shares received by the post.
  • Post Clicks—The number of other clicks on your post (not Likes, Comments or Shares)
  • Negative Feedback—The total count of negative actions associated with the post during the first 28 days. This count is divided into:
  • Hide Post—The number of people who hid the post.
  • Hide All Posts—The number of people who have chosen to hide all posts from this Publisher.
  • Report as Spam—The number of users who report the post as spam.
  • Unlike Page—The number of people who have unliked your page from this post.
