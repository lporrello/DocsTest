Glossary

Account Set

Within , an Account Set is a container for one or more social media accounts. An Account Set is used to assign approval teams for outgoing content, and identify users who are allowed to publish to the accounts.

Activity (Metric Type)

From a metrics perspective, activity is a count of publishing actions taken within (or auto-imported from a social networking site) such as posts, tweets, video uploads and the like.

Alias

Within the Inbox, an “alias” is a unique, temporary name for a stream assigned by an individual user. It is seen only by the user who creates it, and is forgotten once the stream is deleted from a tab. (An aliased stream can be moved from one tab to another, however.) An alias helps a user distinguish between multiple instances of the same stream within a single tab (such as when different filters have been applied to each one, for example, and the user needs to tell them apart). To alias a stream, click on the tab so that the stream is visible, click on the stream’s name in the header, and provide a new name. See also Rename.

Amplify

Within , to “amplify” means to publish (redistribute) content on one of the primary social channels: Linkedin, Twitter, Facebook, or Google+. Amplified content is published as microblogs.

Announcement

(Publish type) When you create certain types of messages using (blog, video, photo, discussion topic) you have the option to Announce the post on microblog channels. In the Advanced filter, you can search for these announcements.

Audience Size (Company Analytics)

The sum of people (Facebook Friends/Fans, Google+ Followers, Twitter Followers, Weibo Followers, LinkedIn Connections/Followers, and YouTube Channel Subscribers) for accounts in scope.

Authenticate

Within , to “authenticate” a social media account means to provide basic credentials (typically a user name/password) so that you can use to take actions (such as posting, commenting, responding, and the like) that you would ordinarily take on the channel itself.

Channel

A social media outlet, such as Facebook, Twitter, or YouTube, also referred to as a Service.

Confirmed Impressions

Confirmed impressions includes members of your network: friends, followers, fans, mentioners, subscribers and the like, who see your published activity. These are also referred to as “direct audience.”

Content Center

Within , the Content Center is a common repository for text and image assets that can be used throughout the company.

Content (Metric Type)

Content metrics consist of data about published activity. Content metrics can be divided into two types: Engagement and Reach. Engagement metrics describe audience actions in response to published content (such as number of clicks or likes received on a post). Reach metrics describe the number of people who have seen, or may have seen, published activity.

Credential (Metric Type)

Credential metrics (also called account metrics) consist of data about a specific account within a social networking channel, such as the number of Facebook friends or number of Twitter followers the account has.

Direct Audience

See Confirmed Impressions.

Engagement

Engagement consists of actions audience members take on the content you publish, such as Likes, Comments, Retweets, and the like.

Inbound (Label)

An inbound label is one that is applied to a stream item from within the Inbox.

Indirect Audience

See Potential Impressions.

Initiative

Within , an Initiative is a project or effort around which social media events are built. Initiatives can be used to group accounts, limit user access to sections of the application, or segment reporting to align with specific social media efforts. Examples of Initiatives could be a product launch, an education campaign, or customer care.

Label

Within , a label is a text descriptor (often a single word, but can be more) that is manually assigned to a piece of published activity or a stream item. Labels help you classify items and find them within .

Microblog

Within , a microblog message is a post in Facebook, a Tweet in Twitter, a post in Google+, a Weibo in Weibo, and an Update in LinkedIn.

OPML

OPML (Outline Processor Markup Language)—an XML standard often used for webfeed lists. You can upload an OPML file identifying multiple blogs you want to include in an RSS stream.

Outbound (Label)

An outbound label is one assigned to outgoing activity, such as labels put on a Facebook microblog or Twitter tweet.

Potential Impressions

Potential impressions consists of people who could have seen your published content; they are people who are friends or followers of people in your direct audience. These are also called “indirect audience.”

Previously Unpublished Posts

A Facebook message that was originally created as an Unpublished Page Post (dark post), then subsequently published.

Reach (Initiative Analytics)

Reach is the total number of people who have seen, or potentially could have seen, your published content. It is the sum of your network members, plus confirmed and potential impressions of your content.

Rename

Within the Inbox, to “rename” a stream is to provide a new, permanent, universal name for the stream that appears to all users in the Initiative (unless the user has provided a unique alias for the stream). To rename a stream, find the stream in the All Streams section of the Stream Library, click on the name, and provide a new one. The name appears for users when they refresh the page.

Response

Within , “responses” are items that are published in response to content that appears in Inbox streams. These are: messages published using the “Amplify” feature in the Inbox, Twitter Replies, Direct Messages, and Retweets (both types, Editable and ‘native’) made from the Inbox, Facebook Private Messages (published through or auto-imported), Weibo Reposts, Comments made from the Inbox on Facebook, YouTube, LinkedIn, Weibo or Google+ Posts. Facebook and Weibo Replies are counted as Comments. For Tweets published outside of , then auto-imported, considers the Tweet a “response” if the Tweet begins with “RT” or if Twitter identifies the Tweet as a response within the metadata it provides (the in_reply_to_status_id value is set).

Restricted (Twitter) Account

A Twitter account that has been authenticated in for the sole purpose of assignment to Twitter search-based and Twitter Users streams. These accounts cannot be used for publishing or for responding to stream items in the Inbox, or for creating Twitter account-based streams, and no metrics data is collected for them.

Scheduled Message

(Publish type) Includes all microblog and non-microblog messages published through .

Social Property

A presence maintained by a company or organization on a social channel, such as a Facebook Brand page, LinkedIn Company page, Customer Care Twitter handle.

Stream

A stream is a view into a collection of search results returned by the system in response to a query. The query defines terms you want to look for (such as the name of your organization or product, for example, or all of the messages that are posted by a particular account), and where you want to look for them (on Facebook or Twitter, for example). The system performs these searches, and presents the results as a flow of data, called a stream.

Targeting

For microblog messages, you can specify that messages be posted to appear to people in a particular geography, speak a certain language, or meet other criteria, which vary by channel.

Unpublished Page Post

Facebook messages that appear only as promoted messages in the Newsfeed. They never appear in the Page’s Timeline, and are often used by Brands to test the effectiveness of their messages, or to compare performance of different message texts without creating multiple posts on the same topic, or publishing too many similar posts at or around the same time
