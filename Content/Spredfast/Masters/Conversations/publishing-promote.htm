Promotion

Pay to promote the message to your fans in the Newsfeed. You can promote to one Facebook Page account at a time. The message is published according to schedule, then the content is sent to Facebook for approval as a promotion. Once Facebook has approved the promotion, typically within 24 hours, it appears in the Newsfeed. If targeting was applied to the message when it was posted, applies the same targeting to the message when it is promoted. If no targeting is selected, the message is targeted to the top 25 countries.

Promotion on Multiple Messages

You can use the Promote feature on messages that are part of a Facebook Series; once the Series has been added, use the Edit (pen) from the Agenda entry to add a promotion to any message in the Series. The Promote feature cannot be used on Facebook messages created using the Repost feature.

The Promote tab appears when the content promotion feature has been enabled for the company in , a Facebook Page account has been configured for content promotion, and the user has permission to promote.

Promote a Facebook Message

  1. Create a new Facebook post or edit a post in the Calendar, Agenda view, by selecting the pen icon (Edit). The Compose tab has focus by default.
  2. From the Account pull down control, select a Facebook page that has been enabled for content promotion in (see Content Promotion). Pages that are enabled for content promotion are denoted by a dollar sign icon.

    Targeting
    Ensure that message targeting parameters are set the way that you want them to be.

  3. Select the Promotion tab.
  4. Click the Promotion slider to turn it on.
  5. In the Budget field, choose a budget amount. You can use partial values so long as they are in whole currency units (such as $20.99, or 322 JPY).
    The currency type is determined by the Facebook settings for the authenticated user.
    As the promotion runs, the amount will be subtracted from the Ad account you have associated with the Facebook page in .
    Suggested amounts that appear in the drop-down menu are based on Facebook’s cost per impression, and ’ calculation of how much you’ll need to spend to reach approximately 1/4, 1/3, and 1/2 of your target audience. (Note that suggested amounts are not available for small audiences.)
  6. In the Duration field, choose 2 to 7 days from the time the message is published. It can take up to a day for Facebook to approve the promotion.
  7. Click the Schedule button to schedule your post. In the Calendar, Agenda view, the post will feature Promotion status.
