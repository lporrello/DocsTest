Accounts

This section of the Facebook Performance Dashboard provides data for Facebook Pages only—it does not include data for Facebook Groups or Profiles.

Fans

The total number of fans of Facebook pages in the chosen Initiatives. The number is the most recent sum; click on the number to see the sum for each page. The graph shows the trend in fan count over multiple weeks; click on a week’s data point to see the count for each page during that specific week. These values are provided by Facebook Insights (page_fans).

Weekly Impressions

Rolling count of the number of times anyone has seen content on the pages in the Initiatives over the last seven days in the date range. The number is the latest weekly count; click to see impressions by account. The graph shows the trend over multiple weeks; click on data point to see the count for each page during that specific week. These values are provided by Facebook Insights (page_impressions).

Weekly (Facebook) Reach

Rolling count of the number of times any one unique user has seen content on these pages over the last seven days in the date range. The number is the most recent value of weekly reach (sum); click to see data for each page. The graph shows the trend in reach over multiple weeks; click on a data point to see the count for each page during that specific week. These values are provided by Facebook Insights (page_impressions_unique (week)).

Engaged Users

The sum of unique users who have taken action in any part of the page: like, comment, share, click on an embedded link in a message or heading. The number is the sum over the date range; click to see counts for each page. The graph shows the trend over multiple weeks; click on a data point to see the count for each page during that specific week. These values are provided by Facebook Insights (page_engaged_users (day)).

Total Negative Feedback

The sum of times people have provided negative feedback (hide, hide all, report spam, unlike page, X button) on pages in the Initiatives during the date range. The number is the sum for all pages; click to see negative feedback data for each page. The graph shows the trend of negative feedback over multiple weeks; click on a data point to see the count for each page. These values are provided by Facebook Insights (page_negative_feedback (day).

Impressions, Reach & Engaged Users

Shows trends in Impressions, (Facebook) Reach, and Engaged Users, side by side, by day across the date range. Click on a data point to see the Impressions, Facebook Reach, or Engaged Users data per account. These values are provided by Facebook.

Page Stories by Type

Shows the ways in which viewers are engaging with pages in the Initiative, by type of story. The number shows the sum for all accounts; click on it to see stories per account. Click on a section of the pie chart (story type) to see the pages in which they were created. These values were provided by Facebook.

Story types include the following:

  • Page Like: a person likes the page
  • Page post: a person creates a story on one of your page posts, by liking, commenting, sharing
  • User post: a person posts on your page
  • Mention: a person mentions your page
  • Checkin: if the page configured as a location and someone checks in.
  • Other: other types of engagement, such as responding to a poll or redeeming an offer.

Facebook Fans

Shows the growth trend of fan size by month, for all pages in the Initiative, over the date range. Click on the name of a month for daily fan data, by account.

Accounts by Engaged Users

The top 50 pages in the Initiatives based on the number of Engaged Users. For each page, provides the number of Engaged Users, the number of Messages, Page Stories, Fans, Impressions; click on the values to see a breakdown for each data type, per page. These values were provided by Facebook.

The Storytellers count is the sum of people who have engaged with the pages in a way that created a story on Facebook (such as liking a page or post, commenting, sharing and the like) for each day over the date range. Use these values to compare the demographics of your fans to the demographics of those who actively engage. These values were provided by Facebook.
