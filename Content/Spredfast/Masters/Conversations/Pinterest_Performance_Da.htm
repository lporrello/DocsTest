Pinterest Performance Dashboard

Audience Size

The sum of audience members (Followers) for all Pinterest Boards in the Initiatives. The percentage change (up or down) is calculated by comparing the data from the current date range to the data from the prior (equivalent) date range. For example, if the date range is 60 days, the percentage is calculated by comparing the current data to data from the previous 60 day period. Click the percentage to see each account’s values for the current period, previous period, change (count) and change (percentage). The graph shows the trend in Follower count over multiple weeks; click on a week’s data point to see the Follower counts for each Board for that specific week.These values are calculated by .

Published Pins

The sum of all pins published through and auto-imported for all Boards in the Initiatives over the date range. The percentage change (up or down) is calculated by comparing the data from the current date range to the data from the prior (equivalent) date range. For example, if the date range is 60 days, the percentage is calculated by comparing the current data to data from the previous 60 day period. Click the percentage to see each account’s values for the current period, previous period, change (count) and change (percentage). The graph shows the trend in published pins over multiple weeks; click on a week’s data point to see the published pin count for each Board for that specific week. These values are calculated by .

Engagement on Pins

The total (count) of all engagement (Repins, Likes and comments) received by pins published through and auto-imported during the date range, for all Boards in the Initiatives. The percentage change (up or down) is calculated by comparing the data from the current date range to the data from the prior (equivalent) date range. For example, if the date range is 60 days, the percentage is calculated by comparing the current data to data from the previous 60 day period. Click the percentage to see each account’s values for the current period, previous period, change (count) and change (percentage). The graph shows the trend in engagement over multiple weeks; click on a week’s data point to see the engagement counts for each Board for that specific week.These values are calculated by .

Highest Performing Content

Top 3 pins published through or auto-imported during the date range that received the highest engagement (cumulative since publication).

Lowest Performing Content

Bottom 3 pins published through and auto-imported during date range that received the lowest engagement (cumulative since publication).

Repins

The number of Repins received by pins published through and auto-imported from all Pinterest Boards in the Initiatives, over the date range. The number is the total for all Boards over the date range; click on it to see the number per Board. This data is provided by Pinterest.

Engagement

The total of all engagement (Repins, Likes and comments) received by pins published through and auto-imported during the date range by all Pinterest Boards in the Initiatives. Click on the number to see engagement totals per Board. This data is calculated by Spredfast.

Comments

Number of comments received by all pins published through and auto-imported, for all Pinterest Boards that are part of the Initiatives. This data is provided by Pinterest.

Likes

Number of Likes received by all pins published through and auto-imported, for all Pinterest Boards that are part of the Initiatives. This data is provided by Pinterest.

Top Pins by Engagement

The top 50 individual pins published through and auto-imported, based on the amount of engagement (count) they received, across all Boards in the Initiatives. (Note that when there are duplicates of a pin in the list, it often means the item was published through multiple Initiatives.) Click on the text in the Text column to see the Initiatives the pin was published in; then click the ID in the Media column to go to the Message Details page. These values are calculated by .

If you want to see more than 50 pins, use the Pin Explorer.
